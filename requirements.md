# AI for Bharat – Product Requirements Document

## 1. Introduction

AI for Bharat is a voice-first, multilingual artificial intelligence platform designed to democratize access to AI across India. The system enables users to interact with technology in their native language, reducing digital barriers and improving access to critical services such as education, government schemes, healthcare information, and small business support.

The platform focuses on inclusivity, scalability, and real-world impact.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Despite rapid technological growth, a large portion of India's population remains excluded from the AI revolution due to:

- Language barriers across hundreds of regional languages and dialects  
- Low digital literacy  
- Limited access to high-speed internet  
- Lack of awareness about essential services  
- Technology designed primarily for English-speaking users  

There is a need for an AI system built specifically for Bharat — accessible, intelligent, and easy to use.

---

## 3. Objectives

### Primary Objective
Build an AI assistant that enables every Indian to access information and services through natural voice interactions.

### Secondary Objectives
- Support multiple Indian languages  
- Provide accurate and contextual responses  
- Operate effectively on low-cost smartphones  
- Ensure accessibility for users with minimal technical skills  
- Drive digital inclusion at scale  

---

## 4. Target Users

### Primary Users
- Rural citizens  
- Students  
- Farmers  
- Small business owners  
- First-time smartphone users  

### Secondary Users
- Government agencies  
- NGOs  
- Educational institutions  
- Healthcare outreach programs  

---

## 5. Key Features

### Must-Have Features (MVP)

- Voice-to-voice conversational AI  
- Multilingual support for major Indian languages  
- Real-time translation  
- Government scheme discovery and guidance  
- Educational assistance  
- Low-bandwidth optimization  

### Should-Have Features

- Personalized recommendations  
- Offline fallback capabilities  
- Domain-specific assistants (agriculture, healthcare, jobs)  
- Integration with messaging platforms  

### Future Enhancements

- Hyperlocal dialect support  
- AI-driven financial guidance  
- Telemedicine integrations  
- Marketplace enablement for small businesses  

---

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

**Scalability:** Architecture must support millions of concurrent users.  
**Performance:** Average response time below 2 seconds.  
**Availability:** Target uptime of 99.5% or higher.  
**Security:** End-to-end encryption for user interactions.  
**Privacy:** Compliance with Indian data protection regulations.  
**Accessibility:** Simple, voice-first interface designed for low digital literacy.

---

## 7. Success Metrics

The success of AI for Bharat will be measured through:

- Monthly Active Users (MAU)  
- User retention rate  
- Voice interaction completion rate  
- Translation accuracy  
- Task success rate  
- User satisfaction scores  

---

## 8. Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
|--------|------------------|
| Language model inaccuracies | Continuous fine-tuning on regional datasets |
| Low adoption | Partnerships with governments and NGOs |
| Connectivity limitations | Offline capabilities and optimized data usage |
| Trust barriers | Transparent and explainable AI responses |

---

## 9. Vision

AI for Bharat aims to become the foundational AI layer for inclusive digital growth across India.

By combining multilingual intelligence with voice interaction, the platform has the potential to empower over a billion people and ensure that the benefits of artificial intelligence are accessible to all.
